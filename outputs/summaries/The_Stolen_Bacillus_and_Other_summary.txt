The man in charge hadn't been about just at the moment, so that he didn't know which bird it was. Bailey could now see clearly that it was one of Fitzgibbon's Orientals, and began to realise what the sinuous thing the man carried in his hand might be. "He didn't come a moment too soon, for I was about sick enough of the desolation of it, and only hesitating whether I should walk out into the sea and finish up the business that way, or fall back on the green things.... "I sold the bones to a man named Winslow--a dealer near the British Museum, and he says he sold them to old Havers. But it was a queer thing to happen to a man; wasn't it--altogether?"
