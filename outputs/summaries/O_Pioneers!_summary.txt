TITLE : O Pioneers!
AUTHOR: Willa Cather
METHOD: textrank

After supper Lou put on a necktie and went across the fields to court Annie Lee, and Carl and Oscar sat down to a game of checkers, while Alexandra read "The Swiss Family Robinson" aloud to her mother and Emil. The pleasantest rooms in the house are the kitchen--where Alexandra's three young Swedish girls chatter and cook and pickle and preserve all summer long--and the sitting-room, in which Alexandra has brought together the old homely furniture that the Bergsons used in their first log house, the family portraits, and the few things her mother brought from Sweden. "Don't you know me, Alexandra? We'd never have bought the place if it hadn't been for this orchard, and then I wouldn't have had Alexandra, either." "Oh, you don't know Alexandra as well as you think you do! I shouldn't think you'd want to remind us how hard you were on us, Alexandra, after the way you baby Emil." "That's just what I don't want to do, Alexandra. When Ivar drove up to the gate, Emil and Marie Shabata began to carry out the presents, and Alexandra went into her bedroom to bid Signa good-bye and to give her a few words of good counsel. You won't feel that we ought to wait longer, on Emil's account, will you, Alexandra?" "Lou and Oscar can't see those things," said Alexandra suddenly.
